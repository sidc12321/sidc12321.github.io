High-Dimensional Changepoint Analysis

This was the work for my Master's Thesis during Part III of the Mathematical Tripos at the University of Cambridge. This work is primarily in a field of statistics called changepoint analysis. The goal is to detect the points where a change occurs in the data generating process. An example would be observing sensor data before and after an earthquake. The goal would be to detect the changepoint as early as possible.

This can be a challenging problem when the change is over a sparse set of dimensions and the data is high-dimensional and missing in a non-uniform way across different dimensions. This work develops a new method for detecting changepoints in high-dimensional data with heterogeneous missingness.

My work extends the work of Samworth et. al (2020) https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.03668 by incorporating missing data and providing theoretical guarantees on the detection of changepoints.
