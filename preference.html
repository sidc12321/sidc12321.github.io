Preference Theory: Deep Dive into the Foundations of Mathematical Finance

My work involved analysing the VNM utility theorem from first principles including conditions for the existence of VNM utility functions, from a more robust perspective than is typically discussed by making appeals to measure theory.

I also looked into several limitations of the theory as a normative economic model including Ellsberg and Allais paradoxes, analysing some of the more uncited examples to demonstrate these.

I developed this further by investigating some more of the recent developments in the theory which aimed to solve these issues, including Choquet expected utility theory and Minmax expected utility theory.
